My name is Harley Johnson. I am an artist and a computer programmer who has enjoyed coding since she was a pre-teen. I am from St. John's, Michigan. I am in my late 20's, looking forward to the greater financial stability that has been said comes with your 30's.

I started teaching myself to code HTML when I was about 11 years old. I learned it from a children's site called Neopets.com, where you could create a webpage for your pet. I thought that was so cool, so I started looking up more and turning the pages into whatever I wanted. I have also been using a computer since I was 3 years old, when my grandpa introduced me to computer games. From then on, I spent a lot of time on the computer through most of my early life.

By the time I reached high school coding classes, I already knew how to make web pages and edit blog layouts. As I learned about VB.net, I soon became fascinated by actionscript for flash animation, making games, and really anything to do with coding. By the time I took a college level programming course half the school day through the whole year, I was able to fly through it. I helped teach the other students and received a "Most Outstanding Programmer" award at the end of the year.

After high school, I became focused on art for a long time. I drew realistic portraits of people. I eventually moved on to animals and drawing in color. I took art classes for a couple years and practiced on my own every day. I have just recently started learning to paint more in oils. Art is a life saver, one of the most relaxing things can be done and one of the most rewarding. If I could more easily make a living on it, I would do so. Fortunately for me, I have other useful talents such as coding, which I'm hoping can help me make a living doing something I love.

Anyway, this is me.
Thank you for reading and getting to know me.
-Harley
